Abstract

PhishLens is a secure full-stack web platform that integrates AI-assisted phishing email detection, explainable risk outputs, and role-based workflows for users, researchers, and administrators. Built using Agile + DevSecOps methodology, the system combines a React/Vite frontend, Express backend API, MongoDB persistence, and a FastAPI ML service. Three transformer-based models â€” BERT, DistilBERT, and LLaMA â€” were trained on 18,000 emails and evaluated both individually and through five ensemble techniques. The best method, Stacking with Random Forest, achieved 98.44% accuracy and F1-score of 0.9805. The platform provides confidence scores, suspicious indicator explanations, RBAC dashboards, dataset upload/training workflows, 2FA security, and comprehensive testing evidence.

Introduction
  • Phishing emails exploit human psychology using urgency, impersonation, and fake authority to steal credentials
  • Attack wording evolves constantly, making rule-based filters insufficient
  • AI and transformer models learn language patterns that generalise to novel attacks
  • Secure design is essential: platforms storing accounts and ML predictions must enforce authentication, access control, and audit trails from the start
Many existing projects stop at model evaluation. PhishLens integrates explainable detection, RBAC, 2FA, dataset workflows, and testing evidence into a single deployable system.

  • Train and compare BERT, DistilBERT, and LLaMA models on 18,000 labelled emails
